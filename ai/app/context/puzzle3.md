# Puzzle 3 — The Star Map: Triangulation

## In-World Framing
The Beacon's navigation array has lost its destination coordinates.
Historical flight logs record routes between known star systems. By
plotting these routes on the star map, a triangular region forms. The
unnamed system trapped inside that triangle holds the coordinates needed
to unlock the next phase.

## Mechanics
- Players read a travel log listing routes between named star systems.
- Each route is drawn as a straight line on the interactive star map.
- Multiple routes from different players create intersecting lines.
- The intersecting lines form a triangle enclosing one specific area.
- The unnamed star system inside that triangle is the solution.
- Any player who selects the correct enclosed system solves the puzzle
  for the whole team.

## Star Systems Referenced in Logs
AT2, Orion 3, SysX, Sys0, Sys1 (named systems).
One unnamed system is enclosed by the triangle formed by the routes.

## Example Travel Log Format
ROUTE 1 (UserA): AT2 -> Orion 3
ROUTE 2 (UserB): Sys1 -> SysX
ROUTE 3 (UserC): AT2 -> Sys1
ROUTE 4 (UserD): Orion 3 -> SysX

## Oracle Hint Guidance

### Tier 1 (vague, thematic)
- Reference ancient navigation and reading the stars.
- Do not name any star system or route.
- Example: "Ancient navigators did not travel to their destination directly,
  Seeker. They drew lines between what they knew, and found what was hidden
  at the crossing."

### Tier 2 (slightly more concrete)
- Ask how many routes the crew has drawn so far.
- Suggest they look for where the lines intersect and what shape they form.
- Example: "How many paths has the crew charted? When lines cross, they
  speak of boundaries. Look to what lies enclosed within, not at the lines
  themselves."

### Tier 3 (mechanic guidance, no full answer)
- Explicitly remind players that the solution is inside the triangle,
  not at the endpoints of routes.
- Suggest they trace the boundary of the formed shape and look inward.
- Example: "The destination is not at the end of any single path, but at
  the heart of the shape all paths together describe. Look within the
  boundary, not upon its edges."

## Key Lore References
- The Beacon last transmitted coordinates from a region near the
  unnamed system.
- Station logs indicate the unnamed system was deliberately excluded
  from official charts — a classified waypoint.
- The triangulation method was developed by Navigator Elara Voss,
  whose logs are partially corrupted.
