# Puzzle 5 — Word Search: The Final Transmission

## In-World Framing
The Beacon's final transmission was encoded as a word matrix. Four critical
words — one from each of the four previous missions — are hidden within the
character grid. Finding all four words deciphers the transmission and
completes the crew's mission.

## Mechanics
- Players are shown a character matrix (letter grid).
- Four words must be located within it.
- Each word comes from one of the four previous puzzles as the final
  answer/keyword produced by that puzzle.
- Words may appear horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
- The puzzle is complete when all four words are highlighted/found.

## The Four Words
Each word is the key answer word produced by puzzles 1-4.
The Oracle does NOT know these words in advance — they are determined
by the outcomes of the previous puzzles in the session. The Oracle
should guide players to recall what they discovered in earlier puzzles.

## Oracle Hint Guidance

### Tier 1 (vague, thematic)
- Reference the connection between the current puzzle and past missions.
- Do not name any specific word or puzzle.
- Example: "The matrix holds echoes of all that came before, Seeker.
  The crew has already discovered the answers — they need only remember
  what each trial revealed."

### Tier 2 (slightly more concrete)
- Ask which of the four previous puzzle answers the crew remembers.
- Suggest they start with the word they are most confident about.
- Example: "What did the crew learn from their first trial? Or their
  second? Each mission left behind a word — a shard of the final
  message. Recall what was revealed, then seek it in the matrix."

### Tier 3 (mechanic guidance, no full answer)
- Remind players that words can run in any direction: horizontal,
  vertical, or diagonal.
- Suggest they search for the first letter of a remembered word
  and trace outward.
- Example: "A word in the matrix does not always read as the eye
  expects. Find the first mark of what you seek, then let your
  gaze follow the line it begins — left, right, up, down, or
  at an angle the Beacon's engineers favored."

## Key Lore References
- This final transmission was composed by the Beacon's AI core before
  it went dark — a message meant only for the crew that proved worthy.
- The four words together form the access sequence for the escape route.
- Chief Navigator Elara Voss embedded the words personally, one per
  mission log, as a contingency.
